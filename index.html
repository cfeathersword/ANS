Sympathetic
"Fight or flight"

Origin: thoracolumbar (T1–L2). Short preganglionic → paravertebral or prevertebral ganglion → long postganglionic. Releases noradrenaline at target organs (with one key exception).

Ganglion NTACh → nicotinic (Nn)
Effector NTNoradrenaline → adrenergic receptors
ExceptionSweat glands — sympathetic but uses ACh → muscarinic M
Adrenal medullaDirect preganglionic innervation → releases adrenaline (80%) + NA (20%) into bloodstream
Parasympathetic
"Rest and digest"

Origin: craniosacral (CN III, VII, IX, X; S2–4). Long preganglionic → ganglion near/within target → short postganglionic. Uses acetylcholine at both synapses.

Ganglion NTACh → nicotinic (Nn)
Effector NTACh → muscarinic receptors (M1–M5)
Key cranial nerveVagus (CN X) — heart, lungs, GI tract
Target organ effects
HeartSymp: ↑ rate, ↑ force β1  |  Para: ↓ rate M2
LungsSymp: bronchodilation β2  |  Para: bronchoconstriction M3
PupilsSymp: dilation / mydriasis α1  |  Para: constriction / miosis M3
Blood vesselsSymp: vasoconstriction α1   vasodilation in skeletal muscle β2
GI tractSymp: ↓ motility, sphincter contraction  |  Para: ↑ motility, secretion M3
BladderSymp: detrusor relaxation β3  |  Para: detrusor contraction M3
LiverSymp: glycogenolysis → ↑ blood glucose β2
Sweat glandsSymp innervation but ACh → muscarinic M (the classic exception)
Key distinction: Both divisions use ACh at the preganglionic (first) synapse onto nicotinic receptors. The divergence happens at the second synapse — sympathetic switches to noradrenaline onto adrenergic receptors; parasympathetic stays with ACh onto muscarinic receptors.

Three parallel G protein pathways — all triggered by adrenergic receptor activation, all producing different downstream effects.

Gs pathway — β1, β2, β3
NA / adrenaline binds β receptor
↓
Gsα subunit dissociates (GDP → GTP)
↓
Adenylyl cyclase activated
↓
ATP → ↑ cAMP
↓
PKA catalytic subunits released
↓
Phosphorylation of HCN channels, L-type Ca²⁺ channels, RyR2, phospholamban, troponin I
PDE inhibitors (milrinone) block cAMP breakdown → mimic β1 stimulation → ↑ HR and contractility without receptor activation.
Gi pathway — α2
NA binds α2 receptor
↓
Giα subunit dissociates
↓
Adenylyl cyclase inhibited
↓
↓ cAMP production
↓
↓ PKA activity
↓
↓ NA release (presynaptic negative feedback), ↓ BP, CNS sedation, ↓ insulin
Clonidine is an α2 agonist — activates this feedback loop to reduce sympathetic output. Used in hypertension, ADHD, and opioid withdrawal.
Gq pathway — α1
NA binds α1 receptor
↓
Gqα subunit dissociates
↓
Phospholipase C (PLC) activated
↓
PIP2 cleaved → IP3 + DAG
↓
IP3 → ↑ Ca²⁺ from ER  |  DAG → activates PKC
↓
Smooth muscle contraction via myosin light chain kinase activation
Prazosin blocks α1 → prevents Gq activation → vasodilation. Used in hypertension and BPH.
Comparison point: Gs (β) and Gq (α1) both increase Ca²⁺ in the cell, but through entirely different routes and with opposite effects on smooth muscle. Gs/PKA phosphorylates myosin light chain kinase to inactivate it → relaxation (bronchodilation). Gq/Ca²⁺ activates it → contraction (vasoconstriction).

Key drug classes acting on adrenergic pathways, organised by receptor target.

Alpha-1 (α1) drugs
Prazosinα1 antagonist. Vasodilation → ↓ BP. Also used in BPH (relaxes urethral sphincter).
TamsulosinSelective α1A antagonist. BPH-focused — fewer BP effects than prazosin.
Phenylephrineα1 agonist. Vasoconstriction → ↑ BP. Decongestant. Used in septic shock.
NoradrenalinePrimarily α1 + β1 agonist. Vasopressor in septic shock.
Alpha-2 (α2) drugs
Clonidineα2 agonist → ↓ central sympathetic outflow → ↓ BP and HR. Also ADHD and opioid withdrawal.
MethyldopaProdrug → α-methylnoradrenaline → α2 agonist. Safe in pregnancy hypertension.
Yohimbineα2 antagonist → ↑ NA release. Research use; risk of anxiety and hypertension.
Beta-1 (β1) drugs
Metoprololβ1-selective antagonist. ↓ HR, ↓ BP. Hypertension, heart failure, angina, post-MI.
BisoprololHighly β1-selective. First-line in chronic heart failure.
Atenololβ1-selective, renally cleared. Hypertension, angina.
Dobutamineβ1 agonist (+ some β2). ↑ contractility. Acute heart failure, cardiogenic shock.
PropranololNon-selective β1+β2 blocker. Caution in asthma — blocks β2 bronchodilation.
Beta-2 (β2) drugs
SalbutamolSABA — rapid bronchodilation. Reliever inhaler in asthma.
SalmeterolLABA — 12-hour duration. Maintenance inhaler in asthma/COPD.
FormoterolLABA — fast-acting as well as long-acting. Reliever and preventer.
Ritodrineβ2 agonist → uterine relaxation. Tocolytic to delay premature labour.
Beta-3 (β3) drugs
Mirabegronβ3 agonist. Relaxes detrusor → ↑ bladder capacity. Overactive bladder. Alternative to antimuscarinics (avoids dry-mouth side effects).
Non-selective / other
AdrenalineAll adrenergic receptors. Anaphylaxis (IM 1:1000), cardiac arrest (IV 1:10000).
MilrinonePDE inhibitor — prevents cAMP breakdown → mimics β1 stimulation. Acute decompensated heart failure.
IvabradineHCN channel blocker → ↓ If current → ↓ HR only. No effect on contractility or BP. Angina, heart failure.
CarvedilolNon-selective β blocker + α1 blocker. ↓ HR + vasodilation. Chronic heart failure, hypertension.
Selectivity matters: β1-selective blockers (metoprolol, bisoprolol, atenolol) are preferred over non-selective ones (propranolol) in asthmatic patients — blocking β2 would cause bronchoconstriction. Selectivity is relative; at high doses even "selective" agents cross over to β2.
